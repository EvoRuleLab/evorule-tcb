This document describes the eligibility criteria, terms, and
application process for **free commercial exemption** from
the AGPL-3.0 obligations for evorule-tcb. The free exemption
is a commercial license (as described in
`COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md`) granted at no monetary cost to
qualifying institutions.

The exemption is offered in the spirit of supporting
non-commercial, research, educational, and public-interest
uses of evorule-tcb. It is a discretionary benefit and may
be modified or withdrawn at any time, subject to the terms
of any active exemption agreement.

## 1. Background and Purpose

The AGPL-3.0 license under which evorule-tcb is distributed
requires users that offer the software as a network service
to release the complete corresponding source code to all
users. Some institutions whose mission is incompatible with
this source-release requirement may still benefit from using
evorule-tcb, but cannot reasonably adopt the AGPL-3.0
license for that use.

The free commercial exemption is a low-friction way for
such institutions to use evorule-tcb under commercial
license terms without the AGPL-3.0 source-release
requirement, and without paying a license fee.

The exemption is **not** a waiver of copyright. All rights
in evorule-tcb remain with the Licensor (or its successor
entity). The exemption is a license grant, subject to the
terms in this document and in the underlying commercial
license template (`COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md`).

## 2. Eligibility (Who May Apply?)

To be eligible for the free commercial exemption, the
applicant must satisfy **both** an institution-type
criterion (Section 2.1) and a use-case criterion (Section
2.2). The Licensor reserves the right to waive either
criterion in exceptional circumstances at its sole
discretion.

### 2.1 Institution Type (At Least One of the Following)

The applicant must be one of:

- A **registered non-profit organization** (charity,
  foundation, or similar) recognized as such by the
  competent authority in its jurisdiction of
  registration.
- A **public educational institution** (school, college,
  university) funded primarily by government or by
  public-tuition sources.
- A **government agency or intergovernmental
  organization** at the national, regional, or local
  level.
- An **open-source software project** that operates on
  a not-for-profit basis and whose primary purpose is
  to produce, maintain, or distribute free software.
- An **academic research group** at an institution that
  itself qualifies under one of the categories above.

For-profit companies, including for-profit subsidiaries
of non-profit parent organizations, do not qualify under
Section 2.1 unless they satisfy a separate criterion
specifically designated for them (currently none; this
policy may be amended to add such criteria).

### 2.2 Use Case (All of the Following Must Be Met)

The intended use of evorule-tcb must satisfy all of the
following:

- **Non-commercial primary purpose**: the use must not
  be primarily intended to generate revenue for a
  for-profit entity, nor to support a service that is
  marketed or sold to commercial customers. Charging
  cost-recovery fees (e.g. for training or support)
  does not in itself disqualify a use, but the
  revenue must not exceed the direct cost of delivery.
- **Public-benefit orientation**: the use must produce
  a public benefit, such as advancing research,
  education, public policy, humanitarian work, or
  free-software development.
- **No competitive substitution**: the use must not
  directly substitute for a commercial offering of
  evorule-tcb or of a derivative product by the
  Licensor or a paying customer. The use must not
  cause material commercial harm to the Licensor's
  business.
- **No sublicensing for commercial gain**: the
  applicant must not sublicense the rights granted
  under the exemption to a third party for that third
  party's commercial use.

## 3. Terms and Restrictions

The free commercial exemption, when granted, carries the
following terms and restrictions.

### 3.1 Rights Granted

The applicant receives a commercial license covering
evorule-tcb on the same terms as the standard commercial
license (`COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md`), modified as follows:

- The license fee is zero for the term specified in
  the exemption grant letter.
- The Licensee is not required to pay the subscription
  fee for the duration of the exemption.
- All other terms of `COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md` apply,
  except as expressly modified by the exemption grant
  letter.

The exemption is **non-exclusive** and may be granted to
multiple applicants concurrently.

### 3.2 Reserved Obligations and Restrictions

The applicant must:

- **Comply with all restrictions** in Section 1.2 of
  `COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md` (no redistribution of source
  as a competing product, no reverse engineering for
  competitive purposes, no trademark use without
  separate license, compliance with export laws, no
  removal of notices).
- **Provide attribution** in all materials produced
  using evorule-tcb under the exemption. The
  attribution must state: "This project uses
  evorule-tcb (https://github.com/EvoRuleLab/evorule-tcb)
  under a free commercial exemption. evorule-tcb is
  (c) 2026 DAMU ZHENG."
- **Report changes in eligibility**: the applicant
  must promptly notify the Licensor if its
  institution-type or use-case ceases to satisfy the
  eligibility criteria in Section 2.
- **Submit an annual confirmation**: the applicant
  must, on each anniversary of the exemption grant
  date, submit a brief written confirmation that the
  use continues to qualify under Section 2. Failure
  to submit the annual confirmation is grounds for
  revocation of the exemption.

### 3.3 Term and Termination

The exemption is granted for a term specified in the
exemption grant letter (typically 12 months), renewable on
the same conditions.

The Licensor may terminate the exemption at any time for
cause, including but not limited to:

- The applicant ceases to satisfy the eligibility
  criteria in Section 2.
- The applicant breaches any obligation in Section 3.2.
- The applicant submits materially false or misleading
  information in its application.

Upon termination, the applicant must promptly cease all
use of evorule-tcb under the exemption, and must either
(1) take action to bring the use under the AGPL-3.0
license, or (2) negotiate a paid commercial license with
the Licensor.

## 4. Application Process (5 Steps)

### Step 1: Prepare the Following Materials

- A copy of the institution's registration document
  (or equivalent proof of non-profit / public /
  research status).
- A short (1-2 page) description of the intended use
  of evorule-tcb, including:
  - The project or activity the use supports.
  - The number of anticipated users (internal and
    external).
  - Whether the use will be offered as a network
    service, and if so, to whom.
  - The source of any funding for the project.
- A signed statement confirming that the use satisfies
  all the criteria in Section 2.2.

### Step 3: Review and Follow-Up

The Licensor will acknowledge receipt within 10 business
days and may request additional information or a short
video call to clarify the intended use. Review is
typically completed within 30 business days of receiving
a complete application.

### Step 4: Sign the Exemption Grant

If the application is approved, the Licensor issues an
exemption grant letter, which:

- Identifies the applicant and the scope of use.
- States the term of the exemption.
- Restates the obligations in Section 3.2.
- Is signed by both parties.

The applicant counter-signs and returns the letter to
the Licensor, at which point the exemption takes effect.

For some advanced features of evorule-tcb, a license
key may be required to enable those features. If so,
the Licensor will issue the key in Step 5. Use of
advanced features is otherwise governed by the underlying
commercial license.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. I am already using evorule-tcb under AGPL-3.0. Do I
still need to apply for the free commercial exemption?**

Only if you intend to use evorule-tcb under commercial
license terms (e.g. you wish to avoid the AGPL-3.0
source-release requirement for a network service). If
your existing use under AGPL-3.0 is in full compliance
with that license, you do not need an exemption.

**Q2. My organization is a non-profit, but we also charge
for consulting services. Does that disqualify us?**

Not necessarily. Section 2.2 prohibits a use whose
**primary purpose** is commercial, but does not
disqualify an organization that earns a portion of its
revenue from consulting or training services. The
Licensor reviews each application on a case-by-case
basis. Disclose all material revenue sources in your
application so that the Licensor can make an informed
decision.

**Q3. Can the exemption be used by multiple affiliated
institutions?**

The exemption is granted to a specific applicant. If
you want multiple affiliated institutions to be covered,
list them in the application and the Licensor will
consider whether to extend the exemption. The
exemption does not automatically extend to subsidiaries,
parent organizations, or sister organizations.

**Q4. Can a commercial enterprise obtain the exemption
through a donation?**

No. Eligibility is based on institution type and use
case, not on financial contribution. A commercial
enterprise that wishes to use evorule-tcb commercially
should pursue a standard commercial license
(`COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md`).

**Q5. If my organization changes to a for-profit
structure, does the exemption automatically terminate?**

Yes. The applicant must promptly notify the Licensor of
any change in institution type (Section 2.1) or use case
(Section 2.2). The exemption is automatically
terminated as of the date of the change, and the
applicant must either transition to AGPL-3.0 use or
negotiate a standard commercial license.
